About DocuScope

DocuScope is an application under development that is used to analyze rhetorical features of a text.

The DocuScope project began in 1998 as a result of a collaboration between David Kaufer and Suguru Ishizaki at Carnegie Mellon University. The application is still currently under development, and this guide reflects the latest version - 1.7.6.

DocuScope is an English digital dictionary and visualization enviornment that tags texts for the variety of experiences a wrtiter can create for the reader. These experiences include all the entities an author can capture on the page for a reader: human characters, narrative action, private and public information, instructions, description, reasoning, positive and negative sentiment, and many, many more.
